Manifesto

No one is just a patient.

They are a father who danced barefoot in the kitchen.

A mother who never forgot your birthday — until now.

A teacher, an artist, a friend, a life fully lived.


But Alzheimer's doesn't care about stories.

It erases slowly. Silently.

It takes names, places, voices —

until even the familiar becomes foreign.


We won't let that be the end.


At Reteena Remembrance,

we build technology that helps people hold on.

To their stories. Their laughter. Their humanity.


Through language models, memory graphs, and context-aware care,

we help piece together what disease tries to take apart.


Not as data.

But as moments.

As meaning.

As the thread that holds someone's life together.


We believe memory is more than recall.

It's identity.

And every identity is worth preserving.


For the ones still here.

For the ones slowly fading.

And for the families who love them through it all.


Because every story deserves to be remembered.
